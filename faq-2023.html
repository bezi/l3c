  • The daisy chain RS-485 network for my industrial controls isn't cutting it any more. I want more data throughput but can't replace my two wire cable! Help!
  • Boy howdy, what an unfortunate situation. Not to worry, we've got a solution to suggest. Upgrade to the modern world of Ethernet! Over my existing two wire cable? You bet! The IEEE 802.3cg standard establishes 10BASE-T1L as a physical layer solution to provide 10Mbps capacity at up to 1km distances over two wire cable! The insertion and return loss of your cable will impact your maximum distance so don't expect 1km over crappy 28AWG non-twisted pair cable but if you do your homework, T1L can be a fantastic way to utilize existing cabling to move from limited RS-485 solutions to the modern world of Ethernet at incredible distances. Not to mention, being standards compliant means you're more likely to be able to plug and play with other 10BASE-T1L standards compliant devices! T1L PHYs and MAC/PHYs exist from multiple semiconductor providers, but we would suggest the ADIN1100, ADIN1110, and ADIN2111 from Analog Devices (we're not biased at all).
